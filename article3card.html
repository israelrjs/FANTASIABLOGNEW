2020 Bucket List Ideas

Article by Fantasia Wesley Monday, December 30th, 2019 1:29PM CST




Have you felt incomplete lately? Have you been wanting to receive a sense of excitement, or an adrenaline rush? What about having the satisfaction of saying you have done something? I know exactly how you feel. I remember thinking that my life was boring, and that I didn’t have anything to live forward to. That. Is. A. Lie. Never assume that your life is over and that you can’t do it. You can. It only takes your mind to process and understand that. You need to train it.


My bucket list has been growing extremely long for the last few years, and I have slowly been checking them off of my list. Now, I have bigger and better ideas that I wanted to share with you, and maybe we could do them together! Some of these categories in the list will be dangerous, so be aware. Remember, don’t do any of these things at home without the supervision of a professional of some sort.

1. Complete a Vision Board



I believe in Self-Fulfilling Prophecies. If you write it down, you will most likely accomplish it. According to Psychologist Gael Wood, If You Don’t Write Your Goals Down, You Might Not Achieve Them. Being that I study psychology, I believe in this particular theory because it has worked for me. When I write my goals down, I have the sense of responsibility to cross it off of my list. If I think about what I need/ want to do, it becomes a faint thought to me, and I later forget to do it. Gael said it best, “If you simply think about a goal, you are using the right side of your brain, your imagination. When you write down a goal you are using the left side of your brain, the logical side”. Just take some time out of your day, about 2 hours of it, and think of what you’re trying to accomplish this year. Will it be ridding dept? Saving up? Dream Vacation? Whatever it may be, make sure you write it down. This is the Affirmation for your life.

2. Sky Dive



Now Skydiving is VERY risky. Just the thought of skydiving can give you an adrenaline rush. I personally have an infatuation with being free to the point of danger. I am personally not recommending this if you are afraid of heights or if you are trying to overcome a fear of skydiving simply because you have to be mentally prepared to jump as much as your body. This not only gives you the rush that you might be craving, but something to look forward to in the future. Go skydiving in Dubai if possible, the view is undefeated. But you have to follow through with it. Maybe you can mentally prepare yourself by going indoor- sky diving first.

3. Learn a New Skill



Learning a New Skill can potentially be lifesaving. It can demolish that boredom you have been aching to get rid of. With learning new skills, it doesn’t have to be something boring, but something that piques your interest. You know, one of those “I’ve been thinking” ideas. Maybe, you’ve always wanted to learn how to play poker, or learn how to ice skate. Or maybe you wanted how to speak in public. You can make anything a skill, as long as you put your mind into it. According to a study done at CCSU Business and Development, your brain chemistry changes, you’re learning speed increases, you can make connections and etc.

4. Work Out



This is normally everyone’s New Year’s Resolution. When you go to the gym during January, its packed with people you’ve never seen before. Well, the key is consistency. You can go to the gym once, and never think about it again. Now, make the bucket list say that you are trying to stay consistent. Try going to the gym at least 3 times a week.

5. Shoot A Gun (with training)



If you are pro-gun, or pro protection, learning how to shoot a gun could be a learning experience. Not only a learning experience, but a lifesaving experience. With the latest chaos that seems to be extremely deadly, it’s better to be able to protect yourself in the moment and carry gun knowledge. It may be a scary first time because I was scared when I shot my first gun, but as you practice you gain confidence. Please make sure you are in a insured, certified gun range that will be able to properly assist and teach you.

6. Drifting



Drifting is when you can drag a car in such angles that it burns tires. If you are a adrenaline junky, this may be the hobby for you. You can go in circles with sharp turns as you pull the emergency break and let the tires lock. This could become an interesting hobby depending on how well you are. Make sure you do this with a trained professional that will be able to properly assist you.

7. Hang Gliding



Hang gliding is an air sport that requires you to not be afraid of heights. If you’re trying to overcome the fear of heights, this may be the sport for you. This is a non-motorized, foot launching off of a cliff, risky but free experience.

8. Make a Song



If you believe that you can write a rap, harmonize on a beat, or do it for simple fun, this may be a great way to start off the year. Call your local music studio and try to record a song with a beat provided by them or if you’re courageous, make your own beat and bring it to the studio.

9. Write a Poem



Depending if you’re a profound personality or mentality, you can definitely write a deep poem that may touch others. Write how you feel, go to your local open mic session and let your emotions flow.

10. Parasailing



Parasailing is a bit different from hang gliding, simply because you are in a parachute, flying in the sky, over the ocean, off of a boat. Not the best explanation I know, but just search it up, and give it a go!
